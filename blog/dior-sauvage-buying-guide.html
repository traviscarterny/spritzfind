Dior Sauvage Buying Guide

Updated March 2026 · Complete comparison of all 4 versions
Dior Sauvage Collection

Dior Sauvage is the world's best-selling men's fragrance — but with four different versions (EDT, EDP, Parfum, and Elixir), choosing the right one can be confusing. Here's a complete breakdown.

Sauvage EDT (Eau de Toilette)

Retail: $115 (3.4oz)
Discount: $82 - $98
Longevity: 6-8 hours
Best for: Spring, Summer, Daily

The original and most popular version. Fresh, peppery bergamot with Ambroxan. The most versatile — works at the office, on dates, and everywhere in between. This is the one to buy if you're getting your first Sauvage.

Sauvage EDP (Eau de Parfum)

Retail: $130 (3.4oz)
Discount: $95 - $115
Longevity: 8-10 hours
Best for: Fall, Winter, Evening

Warmer and spicier than the EDT. Adds vanilla and star anise to the mix. Better projection and longevity. Choose this if you want something that lasts through a full work day and into the evening.

Sauvage Parfum

Retail: $150 (3.4oz)
Discount: $115 - $135
Longevity: 10-12 hours
Best for: All seasons, Signature scent

The most refined and smooth version. Less sharp than EDT, less sweet than EDP. A sophisticated, "skin scent" quality that smells expensive. Many consider this the best-smelling version of the four.

Sauvage Elixir

Retail: $165 (2oz)
Discount: $125 - $150
Longevity: 12+ hours
Best for: Cold weather, Evening, Special occasions

The nuclear option. Intense, dark, and powerful with cinnamon, nutmeg, and licorice. Two sprays can last all day and night. Not for everyday wear — this is your going-out fragrance for colder months.

Which Should You Buy?

First bottle: EDT. It's the most versatile, easiest to wear, and cheapest.

Best value: EDT on discount sites at $82-98.

Best performance: Parfum. Longest-lasting of the wearable versions with the smoothest scent profile.

Best for winter: Elixir. Nothing projects in cold weather like Elixir.
