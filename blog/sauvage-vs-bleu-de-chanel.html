Dior Sauvage vs Bleu de Chanel

Updated March 2026 · Head-to-head comparison
Sauvage vs Bleu de Chanel

These are the two biggest names in men's fragrance, and every cologne enthusiast has been asked: "Sauvage or Bleu?" Here's a detailed breakdown to help you decide.

The Quick Answer

Choose Sauvage if you want a bold, versatile crowd-pleaser that performs in any situation. Choose Bleu de Chanel if you prefer something more refined, subtle, and sophisticated.

Scent Profile

Sauvage EDT: Opens with a burst of peppery bergamot, then settles into a clean, magnetic Ambroxan base. It's bold, fresh, and slightly sweet. You'll smell it from across the room.

Bleu de Chanel EDP: Opens with citrus and mint, develops into a woody, slightly incense-like heart. More complex and layered than Sauvage. Sits closer to the skin with an air of quiet confidence.

Performance Comparison

Category
Sauvage EDT
Bleu de Chanel EDP

Longevity: Sauvage EDT lasts 6-8 hours. Bleu de Chanel EDP lasts 7-9 hours. In EDP versions, Sauvage pulls ahead at 8-10 hours.

Projection: Sauvage projects significantly more. People will notice you wearing it. Bleu de Chanel is more of a "lean in closer" scent.

Versatility: Both work year-round, but Sauvage is better in warm weather and Bleu is better in professional settings.

Price Comparison

Sauvage EDT 3.4oz: Retail $115
Discount: $82 - $98
Bleu de Chanel EDP 3.4oz: Retail $150
Discount: $120 - $140

Sauvage is the better value. It's cheaper at retail and goes on deeper discounts. Chanel rarely offers significant discounts on their fragrances.

The Verdict

Buy Sauvage if: You want maximum compliments, better value, and a versatile daily driver. You're in your 20s-30s or want something that projects.

Buy Bleu de Chanel if: You prefer understated elegance, work in a corporate environment, or want something that feels more mature and sophisticated.

Buy both if: Use Sauvage for casual/social situations and Bleu de Chanel for professional/formal ones. They complement each other perfectly.
